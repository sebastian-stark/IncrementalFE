\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\subsubsection*{template$<$unsigned int spacedim$>$\newline
class incremental\+F\+E\+::\+Psi\+Elastic\+Plastic\+Material00$<$ spacedim $>$}

Class defining a domain related scalar functional with the integrand

$h^\Omega_\rho = \dfrac{\lambda}{2} \left[\mathrm{tr}\left(\boldsymbol{E}^\mathrm{e}\right)\right]^2 + \mu\mathrm{tr}\left[\left(\boldsymbol{E}^\mathrm{e}\right)^2\right]$,

where

$ \boldsymbol{E}^\mathrm{e} = \boldsymbol{Q} \cdot \left(\boldsymbol{E} + \dfrac{1}{2} \boldsymbol{I} \right) \cdot \boldsymbol{Q} - \dfrac{1}{2} \mathbf{I} $

and

$ \boldsymbol{E} = \dfrac{1}{2} ( \boldsymbol{F}^\top \cdot \boldsymbol{F} - \boldsymbol{I} ) $

Here, $\boldsymbol{F}$ is the deformation gradient, $\boldsymbol{Q}$ the inverse of the plastic right stretch tensor, and $\lambda$ and $\mu$ are Lame\textquotesingle{}s constants. The scalar functional describes the strain energy of an elastic plastic material, with elastic strains assumed to be small and plastic strains large. The ansatz is based on the multiplicative split $ \boldsymbol{F} = \boldsymbol{F}^\mathrm{e} \cdot \boldsymbol{Q}^{-1} $ of the deformation gradient, where $\boldsymbol{F}^\mathrm{e}$ is the elastic deformation contribution, which is, without loss of generality, assumed to include a rotation part.
